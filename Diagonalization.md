# Diagonalization (对角化)

Proposition 4.3.2.  If $`A`$ is an $`n \times n`$ matrix and there is a basis <br>
$`\{v_{1}, v_{2}, v_{3}, ..., v_{n}\}, `$ of $`\mathbb{R}^n`$ consisting of eigenvectors <br>
of $`A`$ having associated eigenvalues $`\lambda_{1}, \lambda_{2}, \lambda_{3}, ..., \lambda_{n}`$, <br>
then we can write $`A = PDP^{-1}`$ where $`D`$ is the **diagonal matrix** <br>
whose diagonal entries are the **eigenvalues** of $`A`$ <br>

$`D = \begin{bmatrix}
   \lambda_{1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
   0, & \lambda_{2} & \cdots & 0 \\
   \vdots  & \vdots  & \ddots & \vdots  \\
   0 & 0 & \cdots & \lambda_{n} 
 \end{bmatrix}`$
